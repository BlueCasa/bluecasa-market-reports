2026 Market Outlook
Maryland's housing market is expected to remain stable with modest appreciation of 2–4% through 2026. Inventory is gradually improving — up 20%+ year-over-year — but still sits below 3 months of supply, which continues to favor sellers. Strong employment in healthcare, federal government, and education sectors is sustaining demand across the Baltimore and DC suburbs. Spring 2026 is expected to see renewed buyer activity as mortgage rates ease toward the mid-6% range.
Buying & Selling Strategy
Sellers: Spring (March–May) is peak season. Well-priced homes in suburban counties still attract multiple offers and sell quickly.
Buyers: August–December offers slightly more negotiating room. First-time buyers should explore Maryland's SmartBuy and MMP mortgage assistance programs.
Investors: Baltimore City offers one of the most affordable entry points on the East Coast with strong university and hospital-driven rental demand.

Key Market Indicators
DC is the only Mid-Atlantic market projected to see a slight price dip in 2026 — driven by federal workforce changes and budget uncertainty.
Active listings are up 33%+ year-over-year — buyers have more choices than any time since 2019.
34% of homes still sell above list price — well-located, move-in ready properties remain highly competitive.

2026 Market Outlook
The DC metro is transitioning to a more balanced market. Bright MLS projects a modest 1% price decline in 2026 — the only Mid-Atlantic market expected to soften — due to federal workforce uncertainty from ongoing government restructuring. However, sales volume is expected to jump 8–10% as sidelined buyers return. For sellers, strategic pricing and early-spring listings will be critical. For buyers, this is one of the best entry windows in years.
Buying & Selling Strategy
Sellers: List in late winter or early spring (Feb–April) before inventory peaks. Price competitively — overpriced homes now sit for months.
Buyers: 2026 is one of the best DC buyer opportunities in years. More inventory, longer days on market, and motivated sellers willing to negotiate.
Investors: Condo entry points near Navy Yard and east of the Anacostia offer the best affordability-to-transit ratio in the city right now.

Northern Virginia remains one of the most competitive real estate markets in the country with homes moving in under 25 days on average.

Amazon HQ2 in Arlington continues to fuel demand and appreciation in the surrounding area.
Bright MLS projects a 2.8% price increase for Virginia suburbs in 2026, outpacing the DC core market.
2026 Market Outlook
Virginia's Northern suburbs are expected to outperform the broader DC metro in 2026. Low inventory, Amazon HQ2 demand spillover, and a strong tech and government employment base continue to support prices. Prince William and Loudoun counties offer the best value for buyers priced out of Arlington and Fairfax. Mid-Atlantic Virginia markets are projected to see 2.5–3.5% appreciation through 2026.
Buying & Selling Strategy
Sellers: Inventory is tight — well-priced homes in NoVA frequently attract multiple offers within days of listing.
Buyers: Come pre-approved and be ready to move quickly. Escalation clauses are common in competitive Arlington and Fairfax markets.
Investors: Proximity to Metro stations in Arlington and Fairfax delivers premium rental rates and consistently low vacancy.

2026 Market Outlook
Greater Philadelphia is positioned as one of the stronger Mid-Atlantic markets for 2026. Bright MLS projects a 2.8% price increase, outpacing both the national average and the DC metro. A spring 2026 market surge is expected as lower mortgage rates unlock pent-up buyer demand. The Main Line and inner-ring suburbs continue to attract NYC and DC transplants seeking relative affordability and quality of life.
Buying & Selling Strategy
Sellers: April–May is prime season. Turn-key homes in good school districts still attract serious competition and multiple offers.
Buyers: More leverage than in 2021–2023 — especially in the city and outer suburbs. Get pre-approved now for the spring market.
Investors: Fishtown, Brewerytown, and South Philadelphia offer strong rental yield with ongoing neighborhood revitalization.

Buyers: 2026 offers more leverage than any year since 2019 — take advantage of increased inventory and motivated sellers.
